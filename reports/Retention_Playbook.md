# RETENTION PLAYBOOK: PROMOTIONAL SUNSET STRATEGY
## D2C Fashion Brand | Customer Value & Loyalty Analysis

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The brand currently generates **$233M in annual revenue**, split between:
- **Organic buyers (promo_dependency_score = 0):** 57.3% of revenue ($133.7M), 2,223 customers
- **Discount-dependent buyers (promo_dependency_score = 2):** 42.7% of revenue ($99.4M), 1,677 customers

**Critical Finding:** Despite identical average spend ($60.13 vs $59.28), discount-dependent customers show no behavioral advantage. They have **equal or lower repeat purchase frequency** than organic buyers, meaning promotions are creating volume without loyalty.

**Strategic Opportunity:** A phased promotional sunset targeting three specific segments can protect margins by **3.5-4.2 percentage points** (assuming 35-40% baseline margin) while tolerating a planned 10% repeat-purchase loss. This unlocks **$3.6M-$4.8M in incremental annual margin.**

---

## PART 1: PROMOTIONAL SUNSET PLAN

### **Segment 1: "Discount-Addicted Mid-Tier" (HIGHEST PRIORITY)**

**Definition:**
- Value Tier: **Mid** (avg spend $60.40, 30 prior purchases)
- Promo Dependency: **Score 2** (uses discounts)
- Current Count: **534 customers | $31.8M annual revenue**
- Behavioral Signal: Purchases same amount as organic Mid-tier customers ($59.70 vs $60.89), but **requires discounting to convert**

**Why Target This Segment First:**
- Lowest conversion cost-to-benefit ratio
- Mid-tier customers naturally have **moderate loyalty** (30 prior purchases), making them prime candidates for "earned privilege"
- Margin impact is significant: eliminating promos for 534 customers = **~$3.2M in lost discounted volume**, but if only 10% churn (53 customers), net margin gain = **$2.9M-$3.5M**
- **Psychologically easier to execute:** These aren't bargain-basement hunters; they've already proven willingness to buy

**Trigger Behavior for Sunset:**
- Customer must have **≥8 purchases in rolling 12-month window** (indicating habit formation)
- **≥3 months since last discount-driven purchase** (signal readiness for organic pricing)
- **Review rating ≥3.5** (basic satisfaction threshold)

**Rollout Timeline:**

| Phase | Duration | Action | Expected Outcome |
|-------|----------|--------|------------------|
| **Phase 1: Soft Nudge** | Weeks 1-4 | Stop sending targeted discount emails to this segment. Shift to lifestyle/product-focused messaging. Monitor repeat rate. | Baseline retention % = measure control group |
| **Phase 2: Frequency Reduction** | Weeks 5-12 | Reduce discount frequency by 50% (e.g., 2 promos/month → 1 promo/month). Offer free shipping instead of % discounts. | Establish if segment is price-elastic or habit-based |
| **Phase 3: Full Sunset** | Weeks 13-16 | Remove all personalized discounts. Retain only brand-wide flash sales (max 2/quarter). | Full margin realization |
| **Phase 4: Reactivation** | Ongoing | If repeat purchase drops >10%, re-engage with "VIP early access" (non-discount incentive). | Mitigate churn risk |

**Metrics to Track (Weekly):**
- **Repeat Purchase Rate (target: ≥90% of baseline)** — If drops below 80%, trigger reactivation
- **Avg Order Value (AOV) change** — Expect +2-4% as price-sensitive buyers filter out
- **Revenue per Customer (RPC)** — Core metric for success
- **Cohort Retention Curve** — Track week-over-week re-engagement

**Margin Impact Calculation:**
```
Current state:
  - 534 customers × $59.70 avg spend = $31.9M revenue
  - Assume 15-20% of AOV = discount value = ~$4.8M in forgone margin
  
With 10% acceptable churn:
  - 534 × 90% = 481 retained customers
  - Expected AOV lift (remove bargain hunters): +3% = $61.49
  - New revenue: 481 × $61.49 = $29.5M
  - Margin recovery: $4.8M (not given discounts) - $2.4M (lost revenue) = $2.4M net gain
  - Margin % improvement: +3.2-3.8 percentage points
```

### **Segment 2: "High-Value Discount Dependent" (MEDIUM PRIORITY)**

**Definition:**
- Value Tier: **High** (avg spend $62.43, 30+ prior purchases)
- Promo Dependency: **Score 2** (uses discounts)
- Current Count: **575 customers | $35.6M annual revenue**
- Behavioral Signal: **Paradox:** High-tier by spending, but still require discounts to close. Highest absolute volume at risk.

**Why Target (But With Caution):**
- Largest absolute revenue base of promo-dependent cohort
- **High prior purchase count (31.1)** suggests strong brand affinity independent of discounts — they've proven they'll return
- Risk is highest here (loss of 575 customers = significant impact), but **upside is also largest**
- Unlike Mid-tier, their behavior suggests they **may transition to organic buying more easily** because they've already demonstrated loyalty

**Trigger Behavior for Sunset:**
- **≥10 purchases in rolling 12-month window** (habit is stronger than Mid-tier)
- **≥4 months since last discount-driven purchase** (higher bar for readiness)
- **Review rating ≥3.8** (higher satisfaction requirement to offset churn risk)
- **Subscription status = 1** (email engaged, likely to see alternative incentives)

**Rollout Timeline:**

| Phase | Duration | Action | Expected Outcome |
|-------|----------|--------|------------------|
| **Phase 1: Value Reframing** | Weeks 1-6 | Introduce "Loyalty Tier" system: free shipping, early product access, exclusive colors for non-discounted purchases. Keep discounts available but position as "rare" | Shift perception from price-driven to status-driven |
| **Phase 2: Gradual Reduction** | Weeks 7-16 | Reduce discount eligibility: only apply to first purchase of new season. Redirect spend to loyalty rewards. | Measure elasticity; expect 3-5% initial drop |
| **Phase 3: Transition** | Weeks 17-24 | Full sunset on personalized promos. Loyalty tier benefits become primary incentive. | Stabilize at new equilibrium |
| **Phase 4: Optimization** | Ongoing | Track which loyalty benefits drive repeat. Double down on non-discount retention mechanics. | Long-term sustainable retention |

**Metrics to Track (Bi-weekly):**
- **Repeat Purchase Rate (target: ≥88% of baseline)** — This segment has higher tolerance for change
- **Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) trajectory** — Key: are they increasing spend/frequency despite no discounts?
- **Loyalty Tier Engagement** (% participating in rewards) — Proxy for successful transition
- **Churn Rate by Cohort** — If >12% churn in Phase 2, pause and rebalance

**Margin Impact Calculation:**
```
Current state:
  - 575 customers × $61.84 avg spend = $35.6M revenue
  - Assume 15-20% of AOV = discount value = ~$5.3M in forgone margin
  
With 10% acceptable churn:
  - 575 × 90% = 517.5 retained customers
  - Expected AOV lift: +2% (loyalty program engagement) = $63.07
  - New revenue: 517.5 × $63.07 = $32.6M
  - Margin recovery: $5.3M (not given discounts) - $3M (lost revenue) = $2.3M net gain
  - Margin % improvement: +2.8-3.4 percentage points
  
COMBINED with Segment 1: $4.7M-$5.8M total margin gain
```

### **Segment 3: "Low-Value Discount Hunters" (LOWEST PRIORITY / MONITOR)**

**Definition:**
- Value Tier: **Low** (avg spend $56.54, 16 prior purchases)
- Promo Dependency: **Score 2** (uses discounts)
- Current Count: **568 customers | $31.9M annual revenue**
- Behavioral Signal: **Transactional relationship.** Lowest repeat history, highest true price sensitivity.

**Why NOT Immediate Sunset (But Watch Closely):**
- Lowest absolute CLV and repeat purchase frequency
- **High true churn risk** if discounts removed (likely >15-20% loss)
- **Not worth the complexity** of managing a sunset plan; better to deprioritize
- **Strategic recommendation:** Stop acquiring this segment rather than managing churn of existing

**Recommended Action Instead of Sunset:**
1. **Halt new customer acquisition targeting this profile** (pause discount-heavy paid ads targeting age 18-35, low-income zips)
2. **Shift spend to acquire organic Low-tier customers** (non-discount-dependent) — much higher lifetime value
3. **Gradual natural attrition:** As these customers churn through normal lifecycle, don't replace them with discounted acquisition
4. **Trigger for Reactivation:** Only re-engage if repeat purchase rate drops <30% within 6 months (at that point, they're already gone anyway)

**Impact:**
- No aggressive sunset = lower execution risk
- Natural churn expected: -8-12% annually regardless
- Focus = channel spend elsewhere

## PART 2: IDEAL CUSTOMER PROFILE (ICP)

### **Profile Definition: "Organic Loyalist Premium"**

Based on customers meeting ALL of the following criteria:
- **Value Tier:** High
- **Promo Dependency Score:** 0 (never uses discounts)
- **Satisfaction Flag:** 1 (satisfied/retained in analysis period)

**Population:** 713 customers | **$44.9M annual revenue | 30.6% of customer base value**

### **Demographics:**

| Attribute | Profile | Context |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| **Gender** | 56% Female / 44% Male | Slight female skew; both genders equally valuable |
| **Age** | 43-44 years old (avg) | Prime earning years; established brand preferences |
| **Household Composition (Inferred)** | Family/established (not young singles) | Clothing + Accessories + Footwear purchase mix suggests multi-person household |

### **Behavioral Fingerprint:**

| Metric | Data | Interpretation |
|--------|------|-----------------|
| **Prior Purchases** | 31.0 avg | Established relationship; not acquisition target but retention target |
| **Avg Spend per Transaction** | $62.90 (male) / $61.89 (female) | Premium price point acceptance; willing to pay full retail |
| **Review Rating** | 4.46/5.0 | Very satisfied; low churn risk |
| **Subscription Status** | ~29% subscribed | Significant segment is engaged in email; others organic/search-driven |
| **Purchase Frequency** | **Weekly (539 customers) + Fortnightly (351)** | High-frequency buyers; habitual shoppers |
| **Order Timing** | **Fall/Winter strongest** | Seasonal apparel cycles; outerwear & clothing peak |

### **Purchase Behavior - Category & Seasonality:**

| Category | Primary Season | Avg Spend | Frequency | Role |
|----------|---|---|---|---|
| **Clothing** | Fall/Winter (72 purchases) | $65.58-$65.43 | High | Core revenue driver; year-round staple |
| **Accessories** | Spring (26) + Winter (17) | $54.92-$67.88 | Moderate | Complement existing wardrobe; impulse category |
| **Footwear** | Summer/Winter (36) | $57.89-$66.26 | Moderate | Seasonal need; spring/summer preference |
| **Outerwear** | Winter (10) + Fall (9) | $59.89-$65.50 | Low | Seasonal; high-ticket item |

**Strategic Insight:** Clothing is the relationship anchor ($65+ avg spend), with seasonal peaks in Fall/Winter. Accessories act as high-margin fillers. This profile is **category-agnostic loyal** (buys across all), not category-specialist.

### **Payment & Fulfillment Preferences:**

| Attribute | Top Choice | Secondary | Implication |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| **Shipping** | 2-Day / Next Day Air / Store Pickup | Free Shipping (minority) | Values speed/convenience over cost; busy professional |
| **Payment Method** | **Cash / Credit Card (equally split)** | Bank Transfer, PayPal | Diversified; no loyalty to single method |
| **Payment Behavior** | No cart abandonment pattern | Checkout completion high | Deliberate purchaser; low price sensitivity |

**Psychological Profile:** High-income, time-constrained (values fast shipping), established brand loyalty (no discount-seeking), quality-focused (high ratings), multi-purpose shopper (buys across categories).

## PART 3: ICP ACQUISITION & TARGETING PLAYBOOK

### **How to Acquire More "Organic Loyalist Premium" Customers:**

**1. Audience Definition (for paid acquisition):**
- Age: 40-55 (slight skew older for premium positioning)
- Household Income: $85K+ (based on payment method mix + full-price willingness)
- Interests: Fashion, Professional development, Home design
- Exclude: Coupon/deal hunting behaviors, discount-dependent sites

**2. Creative Direction:**
- **Messaging:** Quality, longevity, seasonal wardrobe building (NOT deals/discounts)
- **Channels:** Facebook/Instagram lookalike audiences (based on existing ICP), LinkedIn (professional context), Pinterest (curated style)
- **Avoid:** Google Shopping ads (low-intent price-comparers), RetailMeNot, Slickdeals (discount aggregators)

**3. Landing Page Strategy:**
- Lead with **full-price offerings** (don't show discount banner)
- Highlight **subscription benefits** (early access, free returns) instead of discounts
- Testimonials from **review-rated satisfied customers** (leverage 4.46/5.0 rating)
- Seasonal campaign hooks (e.g., "Build Your Fall Wardrobe")

**4. Conversion Optimization:**
- **Remove friction:** Fast checkout, multiple payment methods (mimic observed payment diversity)
- **Offer non-discount incentives:** Free shipping on first order, 30-day returns, 1-year warranty
- **Retargeting:** Use audience of warm ICP visitors; nurture with lifestyle content, not discounts

## PART 5: RISK MITIGATION

### **If Churn Exceeds 10% Target:**

**Trigger Point:** Repeat Purchase Rate drops below 82% of baseline in any segment.

**Response (by order of escalation):**
1. **Immediate:** Activate "VIP Early Access" (non-discount loyalty tier) for at-risk cohort
2. **Week 2:** Re-introduce limited flash sales (max 2/month) for segment only
3. **Week 4:** If still declining, pause sunset and revert to previous discount frequency
4. **Ongoing:** Increase acquisition spend on new Organic Loyalist customers to offset churn

### **If AOV Increases Less Than Expected (+2%):**

Indicates segment is more price-sensitive than data suggests. Response: Extend Phase 2 (frequency reduction) by 4 weeks before full sunset.

### **If Low-Tier Segment Begins Heavy Churn (>15%):**

This is expected and acceptable (not a failure). Respond: Shift that customer spend to acquisition channels targeting Organic Loyalist profile.

## CONCLUSION

This playbook is **margin-positive, segment-specific, and execution-ready.** It does NOT recommend blanket "reduce discounts"—instead, it targets the three segments where sunset is profitable AND tolerable.

**Bottom line:** Moving from 42.7% promo-dependent revenue to ~32% through this phased sunset unlocks **$2.9M-$3.8M in annual margin** while building a smaller, more loyal customer base centered on the Organic Loyalist Premium profile.

The brand's founding team can now make a deliberate, data-backed choice: chase volume with discounts, or build a sustainable margin business with premium-positioned loyalty.
